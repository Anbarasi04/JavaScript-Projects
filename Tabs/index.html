History

Nature has been an essential part of Earth's history since the planet formed billions of years ago. It includes mountains, rivers, forests, oceans, plants, animals, and all living organisms. Over millions of years, nature has evolved through changing climates and natural processes. Ancient civilizations depended on nature for food, water, shelter, and medicine. Forests and wildlife have played a major role in maintaining ecological balance throughout history. However, human activities such as deforestation and pollution have harmed many natural ecosystems. Today, people around the world are working to protect nature through conservation and sustainable practices. Preserving nature is important because it supports life and ensures a healthy future for generations to come.

Vision

The vision of nature is to maintain balance and harmony among all living organisms. Its goal is to support life by providing clean air, water, food, and natural resources. Nature promotes biodiversity, allowing different species to survive and thrive. It helps regulate the Earth's climate and protects ecosystems.

Goals

Nature also inspires people to live sustainably and respect the environment. Conserving forests, oceans, and wildlife is essential for maintaining ecological balance. Reducing pollution and protecting natural habitats are important goals for preserving nature. Together, these efforts ensure a healthy and sustainable planet for future generations.
